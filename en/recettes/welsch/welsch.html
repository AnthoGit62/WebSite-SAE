Le Welsh
Welsh is a specialty composed
toast, cheese sauce
creamy, and crispy bacon.
Popular in the North, it embodies the
rustic charm of local cuisine,
often with fries or vegetables
green. A must-see in the area.
INGREDIENTS FOR 4 PEOPLE
  • 4 tbsp old-fashioned mustard
  • pepper
  • 800 gde cheddar
  • 25 cl dark beer
  • 1 uncooked country bread
  • 4 slices white ham (thick if possible)
  • 4 eggs
STEP 1
  • Cut the cheddar into small cubes.
  • Cut the bread into thick slices.
  • Make sure that at the width level they pass through the welsh dishes.
  • As much as possible keep the crust of toast.
STEP 2
  • Pour a few drops of beer on each toast (really a few drops, keep about 20 cl for the rest of the recipe).
  • Then brush them with a little mustard (about 2
    tablespoons) and place them in the bottom of the dishes.
STEP 3
  • Place a slice of ham curved on each slice.
STEP 4
  • In a large skillet, melt cheddar cubes without adding fat.
STEP 5
  • Stir constantly with a wooden spoon (it makes wires, it’s normal as long as it doesn’t attach to the jumper).
STEP 6
  • When the consistency is relatively homogeneous and it cover the spoon well when mixing, add any beer and continue to stir. Stir constantly so that the beer is well incorporated into the cheese.
STEP 7
  • Once everything is smooth and smooth, add the remaining mustard, a little pepper and stir again.
STEP 8
  • When the preparation is homogeneous, pour it into the dishes to top the tartines with ham.
  • Bake at 210°C (thermostat 7) for 10-12 minutes (golden brown).
STEP 9
  • Meanwhile cook the 4 fried eggs in a pan (it is also possible to make them in the oven, directly on the Welsh, but the yellow cooking is very delicate. That’s why frying is very good).
STEP 10
  • When the cheese is golden, remove the dishes from the oven, place a fried egg on top of each of them, turn a pepper mill and serve quickly with fries and/or green salad.
  • And especially a beer!
