La Carbonade Flamande
Flemish carbonnade is a Belgian dish
comforting, prepared with meat
beef stewed in a rich
beer with caramelized onions.
It’s an explosion of sweet and
savory that embodies the culinary tradition
of Flanders, often served with
fries or potatoes for a
authentic dining experience.
INGREDIENTS FOR 4 PEOPLE
  • 400 g onion
  • 250 g whole smoked bacon (not vacuum bacon)
  • 5 slices gingerbread (if possible homemade)
  • 30 g butter
  • Guérande salt
  • 3 tbsp mustard
  • 1 kg lean braised beef (paleron, gîte, shaft, etc.)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 bouquet garni
  • 1 l dark beer (Pelforth brown for a delicate taste, or Leffe brown for a sweeter taste)
STEP 1
  • Cut the meat into 2-3 centimetres cubes.
  • Coarsely cut the onions and cut the bacon into large pieces.
STEP 2
  • Melt the butter and sweat the onions in it for 10 minutes to soften (fire at mini under cover).
STEP 3
  • Add bacon, slightly increasing heat, stir regularly, trying to keep covered as much as possible.
STEP 4
  • Once the bacon is pink, remove everything (except the juice) and set aside in a dish.
STEP 5
  • Set the heat to maximum and put the meat in the casserole. Stir regularly (do not cover), the meat must brown on all sides, it will end up making a lot of juice.
  • Remove the casserole from the heat, put the meat in a dish, keeping the juice in the casserole dish.
STEP 6
  • Dilute the brown sugar in the meat juice and put on the heat to reduce it by half.
STEP 7
  • Once reduced, put the fire to the mini and put the mixture lard-onions by mixing it with the 'syrup', add meat and re-mix, add bouquet garni and cover with beer between (80 cl and 1 liter), salt very lightly.
STEP 8
  • Gently cover the entire surface with the gingerbread.
  • Simmer covered for 3 hours without stirring, until gingerbread is melted (remove the bouquet after 1 hour or 2 maximum).
STEP 9
  • During 'simmering', adjust seasoning if necessary.
STEP 10
  • If after three hours the juice is still too liquid, simmer again leaving the lid partially open, the sauce should be slightly sticky on the surface but well liquid underneath and should not burn to the bottom.
